# Career Journey

## 2014 — Android & Java Foundations

I started my professional journey in May 2014 as an Android and Java developer, building applications for finance and logistics clients.

This phase taught me the complete software lifecycle—from requirements gathering and development to testing, deployment, and production support.

---

## 2017 — Enterprise Development

At Capgemini, I moved deeper into enterprise application development while supporting Volvo Cars initiatives.

I began taking ownership of larger modules, collaborating closely with cross-functional teams, and mentoring junior developers through code reviews and technical guidance.

---

## 2019 — Performance Engineering

Joining Avekshaa introduced me to performance engineering and large-scale system behavior.

I worked on identifying bottlenecks, improving backend performance, analyzing database hotspots, and building monitoring utilities that provided better visibility into application health.

---

## 2021 — Financial Systems & FX Trading

Joining Nomura marked a significant shift in my career.

I entered the world of foreign exchange trading systems, where reliability, performance, and operational stability directly affect business operations.

My responsibilities expanded beyond development into production support, modernization initiatives, architecture discussions, and engineering leadership.

---

## Today — Leadership, Architecture & Modernization

Today I lead a small engineering stream while contributing to strategic initiatives across messaging platforms, application modernization, and platform reliability.

My work spans technical leadership, system design, production support, developer tooling, and evaluating how AI can responsibly improve software engineering workflows.
